## Executive summary

Not every engagement is a 300-employee ERP program — and a portfolio that pretends otherwise isn't honest. This was a focused, well-scoped customization for a jewellery retailer on **Odoo 19**: a product tagging system matched to how jewellery is actually catalogued (metal, purity, stone, collection), and sales workflow refinements for retail operations. Delivered quickly, on the newest Odoo release.

## Why it's here

Two reasons. First, it shows current-version fluency: this build runs on Odoo 19, the newest release — relevant if you're starting fresh today. Second, it shows how I handle small scopes: tight requirements, clean code, no scope creep. The same discipline that keeps a $50K project on rails keeps a small one profitable for both sides.

## The problem

Jewellery inventory doesn't behave like normal retail inventory. Every piece carries attributes — metal, purity, weight, stones, collection, design — that standard product structures flatten or bury. The retailer needed products taggable and searchable the way their staff and customers actually think about jewellery, without rebuilding their whole catalogue.

## Key custom work

- **Jewellery-specific product tagging** — a tagging layer over the product catalogue so items can be classified and found by the attributes that matter at the counter.
- **Sales workflow refinements** — quotation and order handling streamlined for fast-moving retail sales.
- **Odoo 19 delivery** — built and shipped on the current release; the codebase is public on [GitHub](https://github.com/dhirennarola).

## Lessons learned

- **Small scopes deserve architecture too.** A tagging model designed around upgrade-safety takes the same care at 40 hours as at 400 — and the client keeps benefiting at every version bump.
