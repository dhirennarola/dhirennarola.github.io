Executive Summary

An elevator maintenance company managing subscriptions for thousands of customers relied on a large call-centre team and manual scheduling — leading to missed service visits and slow responses. I implemented Odoo with automated, subscription-tied maintenance scheduling for 35+ technicians and 70 internal users, plus a custom WhatsApp self-service flow for 4,000+ customers. Missed visits dropped 35% and roughly half the call-centre workload disappeared.

The Challenges

  • Maintenance scheduling for 35+ technicians done manually, causing missed service visits.
  • A large call-centre team handling subscription, payment and service queries by phone.
  • No reliable self-service channel for 4,000+ customers.
  • Workforce attendance tracking disconnected from the system.
  • Notifications unreliable when a single channel failed.

Solution Architecture

Key Customizations

  • Subscription-tied maintenance scheduling — automated visit scheduling for 35+ technicians, reducing missed visits by 35%.
  • Custom WhatsApp self-service flow — 4,000+ customers handle subscriptions, visit reports, maintenance requests and payments themselves, replacing ~50% of the call centre.
  • LanaTime attendance integration — real-time workforce tracking from physical devices.
  • Messagebird contact synchronisation for reliable customer messaging.
  • SMS fallback — automatic SMS when WhatsApp delivery fails, achieving a 98% customer-notification rate.
