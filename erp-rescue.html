Signs Your Odoo Project Needs a Rescue

Spreadsheets are back

My Method

How I Rescue a Failing Odoo Implementation

A structured, no-drama process — so you get clarity first, then a system you can rely on.

1

Emergency Assessment & Audit

I review your database, custom modules, configuration, and integrations, then give you an honest, written verdict: what works, what's broken, what's salvageable, and what it will take to fix.

2

Stabilize the Bleeding

First I stop the active damage — the broken invoices, the failing syncs, the data errors — so your business can keep running while the deeper fixes are planned.

I redesign the weak parts of the solution properly — clean module structure, correct process flows, the right architecture — and agree a clear, phased remediation plan and timeline with you.

I deliver the working system with documentation and training, so your team owns it with confidence — and you're never locked to a single person again.

FAQ

Common Questions

Can you take over an Odoo project another partner started?
Yes — most rescue work means inheriting a part-built system. I start with an audit of your database, custom modules, and configuration, then give you an honest report on what's salvageable and what needs rebuilding.
How fast can you start?
The emergency assessment can usually happen within a few days. Stabilization work — fixing what's actively breaking — typically begins within one to two weeks.
Do I have to throw away what was already built?
Rarely. The goal of the audit is to keep what works and surgically fix or replace what doesn't, so you protect the investment you've already made.
What does a rescue cost?
It depends on scope, which the assessment defines. You get a clear, written quote before any work begins — no open-ended hourly surprises.
